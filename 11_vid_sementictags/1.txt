Benifits:

Using HTML's sementic tags can greately benifit both your websites SEO and the maintainability of your code.


They offer a way of structure your HTML in a meaningful manner, making your website more accessible and easier to understand


<header>
<nav>
<article>
<section>
<aside>
<footer>
<figure>
<main>
<time>

<header>: Defines a header for a document or a section.
<nav>: Represents a navigation menu.
<article>: Defines an independent, self-contained content that can be reused and distributed.
<section>: Represents a section of a document, typically with a heading.
<aside>: Defines content that is tangentially related to the content around it.
<footer>: Represents a footer for a section or a page.
<figure>: Represents any content that is referenced from the  main content, such as images, diagrams, code snippets, etc.
<main>: Specifies the main content of a document.
<time>: Represents a specific period in time or a range of time
